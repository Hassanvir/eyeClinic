3-D Retinal Camera

NIDEK AFC-230 Non-Mydriatic Auto Fundus Camera

Fundus retinal cameras allow optometrists to track disease progression, identify conditions, and screen for issues through detailed photographic imaging of the back of the eye.

The NIDEK AFC-230 features cutting-edge automatic functions including full 3D eye tracking, autofocus, and auto small-pupil and blink detection — making professional-grade photography simple and consistent for every patient.

Key Features

Built for precision and clarity

3D Auto Tracking

Full 3D eye tracking keeps the patient's retina aligned even when they shift fixation, ensuring consistently sharp images.

Small Pupil Mode

Auto small-pupil detection means most patients don't need dilation drops — a faster, more comfortable experience.

Stereo Imaging

Auto Stereo mode maintains a fixed offset between image pairs — giving doctors 3D posterior pole detail never seen before.

Peripheral Imaging

Extended internal fixation control enables peripheral retina imaging for a more complete view of your eye health.

Visual Fields Instrument

Map every corner of your vision

The visual fields instrument tests your peripheral (side) vision — the area you can see without moving your eyes. Many serious eye diseases attack peripheral vision first, long before central vision is affected.

Your optometrist uses this test to detect blind spots, monitor conditions like glaucoma and neurological disorders, and assess how much of your visual field may be at risk.

What It Detects

Conditions assessed with visual field testing

Optic Nerve Damage
