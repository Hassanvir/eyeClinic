Optometry

What is an Optometrist?

The Canadian Association of Optometrists (CAO) defines an optometrist as "an independent primary health care provider who specializes in the examination, diagnosis, treatment, management and prevention of disease and disorders of the visual system, the eye and associated structures as well as the diagnosis of ocular manifestations of systemic conditions."

As primary eye care providers, an optometrist's main responsibilities include:

  • Examination of the human eye (other than surgery) to diagnose, treat, and/or refer for any abnormal condition, in cooperation with physicians and other health professionals.
  • Using instruments, procedures or agents to measure, examine or diagnose visual defects or abnormal conditions of the eye.
  • Prescribing, fitting and applying glasses, contact lenses or other devices to correct, relieve or treat the eye.
  • Prescribing, supervising and managing therapy for the improvement and monitoring of visual health.
  • Referring patients to other health practitioners as required.
Eye Exams

How Often Should I Have an Eye Exam?

Vision and ocular health conditions are not always accompanied by recognizable symptoms — there is often an increased risk to the patient if timely treatment is not started. The minimum recommended frequency for those at low risk is:

Infants & Toddlers Birth – 24 months

By age 6 months

Preschool 2 – 5 years

At age 3, and before entering elementary school

School Age 6 – 19 years

Annually

Adults 20 – 64 years

Every 1–2 years

Older Adults 65+ years

Annually

Eye Exams

What Happens During a Complete Eye Exam?

A complete eye exam is the only way to ensure total eye health. It goes far beyond checking for 20/20 vision. Steps you can expect include:

01
Case History

You'll be asked about your general health, medications, working environment, hobbies, and any vision problems you've been experiencing.

02
External Eye Examination

Your optometrist examines the external area around the eye to check for any abnormalities.

03
Internal Eye Examination

Using a slit lamp microscope and ophthalmoscope, your optometrist checks your eyes from front to back. Warning signs of diabetes or hypertension may also be detected.

04
Tonometry

Measures the fluid pressure inside the eye — an important test for detecting glaucoma.

05
Vision Tests

Includes retinoscopy, visual acuity (the wall chart), and eye movement and peripheral vision testing to assess how your eyes work together and individually.

Emergency Care

What Eye Emergencies Can an Optometrist Treat?

All patients with Alberta Health Care coverage are covered for any urgent medical eye health care or emergency care visit at our offices. Our optometrists can diagnose and treat:

Foreign Body Removal
Blunt Trauma
Corneal Abrasions
Welder's Flash / Chemical Splash
Red Eyes
Flashes & Floaters
Double Vision
Eye Pain & Light Sensitivity

If the optometrist is unable to treat the emergency, they will refer you to the appropriate health care professional. For true after-hours emergencies, contact your local hospital or call 811.

Payments & Office Policies

What payment methods are accepted?
Our doctors only accept Debit, Mastercard, or Cash. Payment is due at the time of service. We do not direct-bill insurance to keep prices low — you will receive a receipt to submit to your insurer.
Can I get a recheck of my prescription?
Prescription rechecks are only available for past patients within 3 months of their eye examination, and must be with the same doctor. Please contact your local clinic directly.
Do I need a separate appointment for contact lenses or glasses?
Yes. Contact lens fitting and eyeglasses services require a separate appointment with the Optical department. Please contact them directly — this is independent from your eye exam booking.
Are your clinics inside Costco?
Yes — all our locations are inside Costco warehouses. On the day of your appointment, please show your booking confirmation to Costco staff at the exit door to bypass the regular line.
Are the doctors part of Costco?
No. Our doctors are independent from Costco and the Optical department. As per College by-laws, our doctors do not share profit for glasses, contact lenses, or any other products sold by Costco.
