Office Policies

Prescription (RX) Reprints

Our doctors will always give you your prescription and receipt at the completion of your eye exam. However, if you later lose or misplace your RX and want another copy, in accordance with the Alberta Health Information Act, we need to verify your identity and record your request — to ensure your personal health information is not delivered or released to the wrong person.

In order to verify your identity, we need:

  • Your complete name, first and last, as spelled in the medical chart previously.
  • Your date of birth (as we can have many different patients with the same name).
  • The approximate date and location of your last eye exam.
  • The fax number where you want it sent, including the area code.
  • Your phone number or contact info, in case we need to verify or confirm information (if it differs from the medical chart file).
  • If the request comes from someone other than the patient (e.g. another optical or doctor's office), a signed release consent form, signed by the patient.

Please allow time to complete your request, as the doctor usually has to verify the medical chart before re-issuing the RX.

Receipt Reprints

Due to accounting and insurance fraud prevention requirements, we can only issue one original copy of the receipt, provided at the completion of your eye exam. If at a later date you require another copy displaying "duplicate," there may be extra fees. The same ID information described above is required.

Email Requests

If you prefer to have your RX copy or receipt emailed to you, or if you have any other health questions or requests via email, we must ensure your email is valid and belongs to you, that there is no error in the address, and that we have your clear authorization to email your health information.

To process the request, please first email info@drquan.ca with the following information:

  • Your complete name as spelled in the medical file.
  • Your date of birth.
  • The date and location of your last eye exam.
  • Your clear, express authorization that you want us to email (unsecured) your medical data back to you.

Only once we receive this can we reply to your email with the requested information and send your private health information by email.

Phone & Record System

As required by the Alberta Health Information Act and the standard of care in optometry, we must properly record every patient request to ensure protection of your identity and personal health information, and to ensure timely processing of your request.

Requests can be made in writing (email, fax) or by leaving a recorded voice message in our phone system. Every message we receive is properly recorded and archived — this ensures no errors are made and that we have proper consent to act on your behalf (for example: faxing to a specific number, releasing information to a different provider, changing a referral, or making, cancelling, or changing appointments).

Insurance Audits & Requests

From time to time, your insurance company may conduct a random audit and verify your claims. As insurance companies are not health care providers or health information custodians, we cannot release any information or confirm questions about services provided without a properly signed release consent form, signed by the patient. Please ensure your insurance company follows the proper procedures for claims verification.

Insurance Claims & Verification

After your eye exam, the doctors will give you a unique numbered receipt and RX containing all information required by the College of Optometrists and accounting requirements. Our doctors are required to sign and provide patients with an RX and receipt that are compliant with all regulations. Our receipt is a standard receipt widely accepted by all insurance companies.

Because each insurance company may have different claims protocols, we cannot reissue receipts in a different format for a specific insurer. Unfortunately, some insurance companies have started asking patients additional questions or demanding further clarification, even when all required information is already on the receipt or RX. Below are answers to some of these common requests:

Doctor's qualifications, credentials, title, occupation
Optometry / Optometrist / Eye Doctor.
License, registration, or ID number of the doctor
This is the same 3- or 4-digit number the doctor has already written on the bottom right of your receipt and RX.
Office address & phone
Same location as where you saw the doctor — also printed at the top of the receipt. See our Locations page.
Doctor's personal phone number
Due to privacy, we cannot provide doctors' personal phone numbers to insurance companies. Our office number is on the receipt: 1-888-381-2677.
Doctor's full name
It is displayed on the online appointment booking site when you booked your appointment.
